-Describe how your culture has influenced your values and identity.

Argentinian people are very straight up and because of that sometimes we can come as abrasive and rude. I remember that during the first year of living in NZ people were calling me rude very often for things that I was saying. It was quite shocking for me because I always though that I was a nice and kind person back in Argentina and I remember that those comments were quite hurtful at the time. We have a very clear sense of who we are and our identity and when you come into a new country people don’t really get that as they might not be used to it... So we have to adapt. Back where I’m from it’s very common for people to talk about emotions and how we feel. People do therapy and it’s a normal and healthy thing to do... Here in NZ it’s not that common and people assume that if you go to therapy its because you have mental problems. People here in NZ exercise a lot to feel good about themselves and ‘silence’ those little demons in our heads... and not many people talks about it.
